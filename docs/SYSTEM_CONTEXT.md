# System Context

The Teclab Library Information System exists within an academic operational ecosystem.
### Primary objective:
Transform manual processes into structured information flows.

---

## Organizational Environments

Teclab is a technical institute that offers the opportunity to take online courses without fixed schedules. The subjects of each program are divided into four modules, where each of these modules includes practical activities, self-assessments, an online class, and a reading module. It is important to highlight that it has resources, such as books or articles, that add value to the course. Based on these last aspects mentioned before, the need for its own library and a system that facilitates the process of that entity is developed.

---

## Primary Actors

- Students: They make requests for books and their corresponding returns, logging into the system with their own credentials.

- Librarians: They are responsible for book lending, tracking borrowed books, adding new books, updating and deleting them, and managing the stock.

- Academic Staff: They inquire about books that can enhance learning during the course, whether for use as class materials or for recommending to students.

- System Administrator: They will be responsible for the creation of the system (including the database, back-end and front-end program, and its deployment in the cloud) and its maintenance.

---

## Current Operational Situation (As-Is)

#### *Spreadsheet Dependency:*

The processes of lending, stock control, and adding, deleting, updating, and searching for a book are slow and difficult to execute compared to the same executions in a computer system.

#### *Manual Processes:*

Tasks performed manually are prone to human errors, which highlights the importance and assistance of automation.

#### *Risks from Data Inconsistency:*

As a consequence of the previous points, there may be duplicate data, incomplete records, or failed stock controls.

#### *Lack of Traceability:*

Traditional spreadsheets do not allow for maintaining a history of the processes carried out. This is important for later identifying errors and information that was overlooked at the time.

---

## Expected Operational Improvement (To-Be)

Once the system is put into operation, human error will be reduced, there will be an improvement in the structure of the information, and it will be faster and easier to access the information we need, add new books, update or delete those already stored in the database. We will also achieve better and more organized stock control.
